Who I Am

Architect & Writer

Heritage, Sustainability & Storytelling — Dhaka, Bangladesh

Architecture has always been about more than just buildings; it reflects the stories communities carry within their walls. I am an architect and writer, trained at the Dhaka University of Engineering & Technology, where I earned my Bachelor of Architecture with a CGPA of 3.75, ranking first in my batch.

My thesis, "The Golden Fiber Tales," focused on the adaptive reuse of the historic Adamjee Jute Mill, serving as an act of cultural reclamation and giving voice to a place the city had forgotten.

My work exists at the crossroads of heritage, sustainability, and storytelling. I have shared insights on mosque architecture in Istanbul, contributed to conservation documentation for UNESCO-ICOMOS at Paharpur, and am currently engaged in a vulture conservation initiative with IUCN, with each experience enhancing my belief that design and writing are fundamentally the same endeavor: revealing the unseen.

Honored with the Claymire & Placemaking Bangladesh Excellence Award for Orange's Call: The Shores of Serenity, a project that transformed an abandoned paddle steamer into a vibrant cultural sanctuary along the Buriganga, I approach every undertaking with the same conviction: that a space, like a sentence, must resonate emotionally before it can be comprehended.
