Who I Am

Architect of Heritage
and Sustainability

I am an architecture student at DUET, Gazipur, ranking first across nine consecutive semesters. My thesis centers on the adaptive reuse of the Adamjee Jute Mill, transforming industrial heritage into vibrant, community-centered spaces.

My design philosophy embraces culture-sensitive sustainable architecture using terracotta, mud, and bamboo, drawing from Bengal's rich material tradition. I participate in UNESCO-ICOMOS World Heritage volunteering, conservation work with IUCN, and international conferences from Istanbul to Dhaka.
