Thesis Project — B.Arch DUET
The Golden Fiber Tale
Embracing the Legacy of Jute from the Heritage of Adamjee Jute Mill
There is a silence of abandoned places, but it is not the silence of empty spaces — it is the silence of memory. The Adamjee Jute Mill was, for decades, the largest jute mill in the world. This adaptive reuse project transforms the 22-acre site into a machine museum, Sonali Bag pavilion, maker spaces, community gardens, and a riverfront promenade along Shitalakkhya River. The Adamjee Mill does not need to be preserved. It needs to be heard.
Adaptive Reuse Industrial Heritage Narayanganj Cultural Conservation SDG 8, 9, 11, 12
Academic Project — Published IIT Roorkee 2026
Beyond Bulldozers
Scrap Steel, Participatory Design & In-Situ Upgrading in an Informal Dhaka Settlement
Tejgaon Kunipara — 65,787 residents across 5.5 hectares. Over 200 family interviews identified 25 critical issues. Four design prototypes using scrap steel from nearby rolling mills reduced material costs 40-60%. No one was displaced. The fear of eviction, one of the 25 critical issues, was answered not by a promise, but by a principle: the city's most persistent residents don't need eviction, they need space.
Participatory Design Informal Settlement Scrap Steel SDG 11

Design of a Secondary School
Exploring Learning Environment — 300 Feet Road Purbachal, Dhaka
A 25,000 SF composite (RCC & steel) secondary school where the warm orange staircase acts as the social heart — a lively gossiping and gathering spine. A cantilevered raised terrace provides unobstructed views of open fields and green sporting facilities. The superroof symbolizes unity; the staircase becomes the knowledge-sharing space.
