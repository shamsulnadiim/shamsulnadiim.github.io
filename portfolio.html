Thesis Project — B.Arch DUET
The Golden Fiber Tale
Embracing the Legacy of Jute from the Heritage of Adamjee Jute Mill
There is a silence of abandoned places, but it is not the silence of empty spaces, it is the silence of memory. The Adamjee Jute Mill was, for decades, the largest jute mill in the world. This adaptive reuse project transforms the 22-acre site into a machine museum, maker spaces, community gardens, and a riverfront promenade along Shitalakkhya River, giving voice to a place the city had forgotten.
Adaptive Reuse Industrial Heritage Narayanganj Cultural Conservation
Academic Project
Beyond Bulldozers
Scrap Steel, Participatory Design & In-Situ Upgrading in an Informal Dhaka Settlement
Tejgaon Kunipara, 65,787 residents across 5.5 hectares. Over 200 family interviews, four design prototypes using scrap steel from nearby rolling mills. Material costs reduced 40-60%. No one was displaced. Published at IIT Roorkee, 2026.
Participatory Design Informal Settlement Scrap Steel SDG 11

Semi Finalist
A Living Canopy
Senegal Secondary School Competition — Archstorming — Feb 2026
A culturally-responsive, living architectural system for a secondary school in Djilakh, Senegal. Guided by Leopold Sedar Senghor's belief that culture, education, and development are inseparable. Using bamboo, mud floor, and thatch roofs anchored to existing trees as generators of spatial order.
Team: N M Shamsul Islam · Tanvir Ahamed · Jerin Tasnim Saba · Sadika Alam Sayma
Participant
Regenerating Island
Catalysts of Resilience — International Union of Architects (UIA) — 11 Aug 2025
A century later, Saint Martin's Island has evolved into a floating resilient ecosystem, where architecture, technology, and coral life coexist. Bamboo-steel watchtowers link through cable cars, guiding visitors across bioluminescent reefs and mangrove gardens. Floating habitats generate energy from tides and sun, achieving net-zero systems.
Team: N M Shamsul Islam · Basudev Sarker
